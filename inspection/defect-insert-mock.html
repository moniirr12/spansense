Defect Insertion, Image-First

Based on your sketch: photo capture becomes the anchor of the "add defect" flow, with severity/extent/works set right next to the image instead of buried in a form further down. Each defect also gets a compact, retractable "Element & Defect History" panel so the inspector sees prior comments, photos and cost the moment they start typing — with AI able to draft both the condition comment and remedial works text. Three layout directions below explore where that content should actually live: inline in the row, in a side drawer, or in a traditional centered modal.

Photo strip + caption first, before scoring
Retractable history for this element/defect
AI-drafted comment & remedial works
Option A No modal

Row expands in place, right in the table — closest to your sketch. Nothing else on screen moves.

Option B Side drawer

Table stays fully visible; editing happens in a panel that slides in from the right.

Option C Centered modal

Closer to today's pattern, but reordered: photos first, then scoring, then history.

Your note — "could implement a design without a modal": Option A does exactly that. Options B and C are here so you can weigh screen-real-estate and familiarity trade-offs side by side before picking one.

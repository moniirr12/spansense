Start on the map.  Every structure you manage lives there — it's the front door to everything else in this guide.
1 · The Map

Find a structure

Every structure you manage is plotted on the map, colour-coded by type. Click a marker for a quick summary, or open it fully to start working.

  1. Click any marker to see a quick popup — name, location, span, length, and the year it was built.

  2. Click the popup again (or the structure's name) to open its full card: a photo, current BCI average score with its condition rating, and when it was last inspected.

  3. From that card, three actions are always available: New Inspection, Previous Inspections, and Documents.

Clicking a marker first shows this quick summary

The full structure card — your starting point for everything

Inspection History

Every past inspection, in one list

Open Previous Inspections from a structure's card to see its full record: total inspections, last inspection date, and current BCI average — then drill into any one of them.

  • Filter by type — Principal (PI), General (GI), or Superficial (SI)
  • Search across all recorded inspections
  • Each row shows the inspector, date, and both CRIT/AV scores
  • Click a row to open it in full — or use + New Inspection to start the next one
2 · Inspections

Record a defect, element by element

Once you start a new inspection, pick the date and you'll land on the inspection workspace — every element of the structure listed as a row, ready to assess.

  1. Click an element's row to open it, then choose one of three states: Add Defect, No Defects, or Not Inspected (with a reason).

  2. For a defect, set Severity (1 Minor → 5 Emergency) and Extent (A isolated → E extensive) using the pill steppers — your BCI score is recalculated automatically as you go.

  3. Mark whether Works are required — No, Yes, or Monitor — and add a comment if it helps the next inspector.

  4. Hit Save on the element, then keep moving through the table. Use Save Draft at any point to come back to it later without finishing.

Severity, Extent, Works, and a comment — that's every defect recorded

3 · BCI Scoring

BCIav and BCIcrit, calculated for you

Every Severity and Extent you record feeds straight into the Bridge Condition Indicator — no manual formula, no spreadsheet. BCIav is the overall average; BCIcrit reflects the single worst critical element.

Both scores fall into one of five bands:

Critical
0–39
Immediate action
Poor
40–64
Investigate
Fair
65–79
Monitor
Good
80–89
Maintain
Excellent
90–100
No action

You'll see these same five bands on the Dashboard's BCI Score Distribution chart

4 · Conclusions & Twin View

Two shortcuts, always one click away

While you're inside an inspection, a small floating rail sits on the left of the screen with two shortcuts:

  • Conclusions — write a short summary of the overall structure condition before you finish
  • Twin View — opens a 3D model built from the structure's real span count and condition data, so you can see where defects sit in context

When you're done: Preview the inspection, Save Draft to pause, or Save to complete it and generate the report.

7 · Dashboard

Your whole stock, at a glance

The Dashboard is the first thing you see after the map — four headline numbers and three charts summarising every structure you manage.

  • Total Assets, High-Risk Structures, Upcoming Inspections, and Inspections Overdue
  • Asset Inventory — your stock broken down by structure type
  • BCI Score Distribution — how many structures sit in each of the five condition bands
  • Bridge Condition Over Time — a year-by-year trend across your whole portfolio
